Diabetes Mellitus

Type 1 & Type 2

Guidelines

  • American Diabetes Association – Standards of Care 2024
  • American Association of Clinical Endocrinology – 2023 Algorithm
  • American Heart Association – Cardiovascular Risk in Diabetes

Pathophysiology

Type 1 Diabetes

  • Autoimmune beta-cell destruction
  • Absolute insulin deficiency
  • High risk of diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA)
  • Requires lifelong insulin therapy

Type 2 Diabetes

  • Insulin resistance + progressive beta-cell dysfunction
  • Relative insulin deficiency
  • Associated with obesity and metabolic syndrome
  • Gradual onset with microvascular and macrovascular risk

Treatment Algorithm

Type 1

  • Initiate insulin at diagnosis
  • Basal + bolus regimen preferred
  • Consider insulin pump (CSII)
  • Continuous glucose monitor (CGM) strongly recommended
  • A1c goal generally < 7% (individualize)

Type 2 (ADA-Based)

  • Start lifestyle modification + metformin unless contraindicated
  • If ASCVD → GLP-1 receptor agonist or SGLT2 inhibitor with CV benefit
  • If heart failure → SGLT2 inhibitor preferred
  • If CKD → SGLT2 inhibitor if eGFR adequate
  • If A1c ≥10% or symptomatic → consider insulin
  • Add additional agents if not at goal

Drug Therapy

metformin (Glucophage, Glucophage XR)

Class: Biguanide

Indication: First-line therapy in Type 2 diabetes

Dose: 500 mg twice daily; titrate up to 2000 mg/day

MOA: Decreases hepatic glucose production

Side Effects: GI upset, diarrhea, nausea

Black Box:

  • Lactic acidosis

Contraindications: eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m²

Pearls: Weight neutral; may decrease B12 levels; hold with contrast studies

insulin glargine (Lantus, Basaglar)

Class: Long-acting insulin

Indication: Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes

Dose: 10 units daily or 0.1–0.2 units/kg/day

MOA: Provides basal insulin coverage

Side Effects: Hypoglycemia, weight gain

Contraindications: Hypoglycemia

Pearls: No pronounced peak; once-daily dosing
